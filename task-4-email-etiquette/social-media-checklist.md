## **Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students**



#### **The Do's**

* Do audit your digital footprint: Regularly review your public profiles on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to ensure they accurately reflect your professional goals and academic achievements.
* Do maximize your privacy settings: Take control of your data by restricting who can view your personal photos, contact information, and daily updates.
* Do pause before you post: Assume everything you share is permanent; if you wouldn't want a professor or future employer to see it, do not post it.
* Do build meaningful networks: Use social platforms to connect with classmates, join tech communities, and follow industry leaders in your field of study.
* Do verify before sharing: Always check the credibility of an article or news headline through multiple reliable sources before sharing it to prevent the spread of misinformation.



#### **The Don'ts**

* Don't vent about academic or professional grievances: Never publicly complain about professors, peers, or internship coordinators, as this creates a lasting negative impression.
* Don't overshare sensitive details: Avoid posting real-time location tags, your home address, or your phone number to protect your physical safety and identity.
* Don't engage in comment wars: Refrain from participating in hostile online arguments or cyberbullying; walk away from toxic digital environments.
* Don't use unauthorized material: Avoid posting copyrighted images, music, or text without proper attribution or permission.
* Don't let scrolling disrupt your studies: Don't allow social media notifications and endless feeds to negatively impact your focus, academic performance, or sleep schedule.
